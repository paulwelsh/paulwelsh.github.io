International ACH Part 2

Help in locating the required information on checks from Australia, Austria, Belgium, Canada, France, France, Metropolitan, French Guiana, French Polynesia and French Southern Territory follows.

Australia
The required information includes:

  • A five- to nine-digit account number
  • Bank SWIFT BIC 
  • A six-digit international routing code for the bank (called a BSB number, which stands for Bank, State, Branch). The BSB number consists of a two-digit bank number, a one-digit state number and a three-digit branch number. For example, if the BSB number is 032001, 03 is the bank number, 2 is the state number and 001 is the branch number. Do not include a dash or any other symbols.
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used for eSellerate's international ACH option.

Austria
The required information includes:

  • IBAN
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • A five-digit international routing code for the bank (called a bank branch code)
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used.

Belgium
The required information includes:

  • IBAN
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used for eSellerate's international ACH option.

France
The required information includes:

  • IBAN
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • A 10-digit international routing code for the bank (called an RIB)
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used for eSellerate's international ACH option.

France, Metropolitan, French Guiana, French Polynesia and French Southern Territory
The required information includes:

  • An 11-character alphanumeric account number. For an account number of less than 11 characters, use leading zeros. For example, if the account number is A123456789, use 0A123456789.
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • A 10-digit international routing code for the bank (called an RIB)
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used for eSellerate's international ACH option.
