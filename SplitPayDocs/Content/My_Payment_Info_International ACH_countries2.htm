International ACH Part 3

Entering Payment Information for International ACH

Help in locating the required information on checks from bank accounts in Germany, the Netherlands, New Zealand and the United Kingdom follows.

Germany
The required information includes:

  • IBAN
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • An eight-digit international routing code for the bank (called a bankleitzahl)
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used for eSellerate's international ACH option.

The Netherlands
The required information includes:

  • IBAN
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used for eSellerate's international ACH option.

New Zealand
The required information includes:

  • An account number, which consists of an eight-digit account number and a four-digit suffix. Do not include a dash or any other symbols. For an account number of less than 12 digits, use leading zeros. For example, if the account number is 123456 and the suffix is 0012, the entire account number you should use is 001234560012.
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • A six-digit international routing code for the bank (called a bank/branch number). The first two digits of the bank/branch number are the bank number, and the remaining four digits are the branch number.
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used for eSellerate's international ACH option.

The United Kingdom
The required information includes:

  • IBAN
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • A six-digit international routing code for the bank (called a sort code). If the sort code is less than six digits, add leading zeros.
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Building Society savings accounts cannot be used for eSellerate's international ACH option.
