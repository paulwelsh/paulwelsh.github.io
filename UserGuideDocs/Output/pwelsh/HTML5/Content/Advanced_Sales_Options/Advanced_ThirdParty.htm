Setting Up Third-Party Tracking

You can use the Third-Party Tracking feature to automatically inject tracking code into your store pages. (If you have manually added tracking code prior to using this feature, you should remove that code from your layouts before proceeding here.) This feature works with Standard Layouts and Custom Layouts, and provides interfaces specific to Google Analytics, custom JavaScript, and tracking pixels (more on these later).

Tracking methods can have one of two states: enabled or disabled. A tracking method that is enabled at the publisher level (under Third-Party Tracking) is turned on for all your stores, except those stores in which you have overridden the default by disabling that particular tracking method.

For information on how to use the page tracking settings for individual eSellers, see Web Store: Page Tracking and Embedded Web Store: Page Tracking.

To create a tracking method:
  1. In the Advanced Sales Options section of the Sales Manager, click Third-Party Integration.
  2. In the upper right corner of the Third-Party Tracking section, click the plus icon.
  3. Click New. Additional tracking options appear.
  4. Make a selection from the Tracking Method Type list, and then fill out the settings for that tracking method. (See Tracking Method Type Options.)
  5. Click Create.

Tracking Method Type Options

There are three main types of tracking methods available for you to use:

  • Google Analytics – This is a straightforward way to get Google Analytics working with your eSellerate stores. Setup is as simple as providing the Analytics profile's main ID ("UA-xxxx..."), and indicating whether the profile uses Google's E-Commerce Tracking feature. See Google Analytics Tracking Methods.
  • Custom JavaScript – This flexible option allows you to provide JavaScript for eSellerate to inject into your store pages. See Custom JavaScript Tracking Methods.
  • Custom Pixel – With this option, you provide the URL for a tracking pixel, and eSellerate will inject that tracking pixel into your store pages. See Custom Pixel Tracking Methods.

Disabling Tracking Methods for Individual Stores

A tracking method that is enabled at the publisher level (under Third-Party Tracking) is turned on for all your stores, except those stores in which you have overridden the default by disabling that particular tracking method. To disable tracking methods for individual stores, you can use either of the following approaches:

  • On the Third-Party Tracking page, select the tracking method for the individual store, and then click Edit. Clear the Enabled check box, and then click Save.
  • In the Page Tracking section for the individual store (eSeller), select the tracking method that you want to disable, and then click Edit. Clear the Enabled check box, and then click Save.
