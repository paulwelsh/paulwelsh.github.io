International ACH Part 3

Help in locating the required information on checks from Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, the Netherlands Antilles and New Zealand follows.

Germany
The required information includes:

  • IBAN
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • An eight-digit international routing code for the bank (called a bankleitzahl)
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used.

Italy
The required information includes:

  • IBAN
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • A 10- to 11-digit international routing code for the bank (called CIN, ABI, CAB). The code consists of the following:
    • A one-digit check digit (called a CIN). If CIN is not included, the entire code is 10 digits.
    • A five-digit bank code (called an ABI)
    • A five-digit branch code (called a CAB)
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used.

The Netherlands
The required information includes:

  • IBAN
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used.

The Netherlands Antilles
The required information includes:

  • An account number, which can be either of the following:
    • For a Giro account, use three leading zeros plus the seven-digit Giro account, for a total of 10 digits. For example, if the Giro account number is 1234567, use 0001234567.
    • For a non-Giro account, use a leading zero plus the nine-digit account number, for a total of 10 digits. For example, if the account number is 123456789, use 0123456789.
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used.

New Zealand
The required information includes:

  • An account number, which consists of an eight-digit account number and a four-digit suffix. Do not include a dash or any other symbols. For an account number of less than 12 digits, use leading zeros. For example, if the account number is 123456 and the suffix is 0012, the entire account number you should use is 001234560012.
  • Bank SWIFT BIC
  • A six-digit international routing code for the bank (called a bank/branch number). The first two digits of the bank/branch number are the bank number, and the remaining four digits are the branch number.
  • The currency of the country where the bank is located

Savings accounts can be used as long as they conform to the numbering described above. Savings accounts with internal bank account numbers that do not conform to the above standards cannot be used.
